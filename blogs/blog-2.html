Blog 2 - Microformats

by Hanli Geyser on 20 March 2024

This blog post looks at the various uses of microformats.

Blogs actually have very spesific kinds of microformating, because they have a very specific function on the web. Blogs are dated posts, that follow sequentially. Each post can stand allone, but also forms part of a larger collective.

Blogs posts are articles. They have an author, a publication date, a summary, and the full content. Each is marked up seperatly, as I have done here.

For a blog post, your root class for the article is an <article class="h-entry">

This then has a heading marked <h* class="p-name">

It has a publication date marked <time class="dt-published" datetime="2024-03-20">

An author, either as a <p> or a url, or ideally an h-card marked <class="p-author">

A paragraph summary marked <p class="p-summary">

And finaly to content itself, which is in a <div class="e-content">

The profile page shows how to set up an <h-card>

You can find more examples on MDN here.

Later on, you may want to explore h-streams as well, and combine it with the h-entry.
